A question on periodic orbits for billiards

In section 2.9 of the book “Notes on Dynamical Systems” by Moser and Zehnder, it is proved the following theorem as a corollary of the Poincaré-Birkhoff fixed point theorem: Theorem: On a strictly convex billiard table, there exist infinitely many distinct periodic orbits. Is the result, or a weaker version of it, still true for convex tables?

Seminar in Pisa: Vitaly Bergelson

Thursday 9th June, h 16:00 Aula Magna, Dipartimento di Matematica, Pisa Vitaly Bergelson (Ohio State University) “Ergodic Ramsey Theory: the Meeting point of Dynamics and Combinatorics” Abstract: The goal of this talk is to demonstrate the dynamical underpinning of some old and new results in combinatorics and number theory. The results to be discussed include Schur’s theorem on Fermat equation over finite fields, theorems of van der Waerden and Szemeredi on arithmetic progressions and some of modern developments involving multiple recurrence along polynomials and applications to the patterns in primes.
